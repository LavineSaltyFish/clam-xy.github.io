Abstract

This final project contains: datasets and networks with philosophers and their attributes, like: eras and regions, analysis of attributes of philosophers, analysis of connection among some attributes and analysis of philosophers' thoughts.

  • Which era and region borns most siginificant philosophers? If they went through something similar in their life experience? To find it, networks are created and degrees analysis is applied to find the boom era and so on. What’s more, each philosopher’s page is download and be used to find frequent words and draw wordclouds.

  • If one research field has closed connection with some sepecific theories? If philosophy changes a lot with time going on? To find answer, one weight network and one heatmap is drawn to find the relationship among some attributes: era, research field and research theory.

  • What are the philosophers in different fields or theories thinking about? Do they think completely different? To obtain it, some representative articles are chosen and extact frequent words in them, as well, wordclouds are drawn for better visualization.

Datasets can be download here and explainer notebook is shown here. Below is introduction to our datasets and brief of each analysis, you can also check our detail pages directly here.

Part 2: Analysis of attributes of philosophers:

In this part, the description pages also download using names in dataset. Then one huge directed network is created based on the dataset and description pages although we use the dataset with less names. The nodes are the names of philosophers, if one philosopher A appear on other philosophers' descirption page, which means A has influence or realationship on the other philosophers in some way, then each of the other philosophers will have one out-link to A and A will have one in-link. Below is the huge network with 1395 nodes and 10023 links, we tried our best to make it looks betterüòë:

Then three attributes are analysed separately: by using in/out degree distribution find the most important class of one attribute and the important philosophers of each class. Then extracting some frequent words in various class and draw the wordclouds corresponding to classes in one attribute. You can check the detail in core areas, regions and eras. All of them are shown at pages.

Part 3: Analysis of connections among attributes:

In this part, one weight network is built by using the second dataset with era, theory and field. The nodes are all the classes of these three attributes. If one philosopher born in twenty era prefers environmental theory and belong to science field. Then these three nodes will be connected with each other which creates three links.
